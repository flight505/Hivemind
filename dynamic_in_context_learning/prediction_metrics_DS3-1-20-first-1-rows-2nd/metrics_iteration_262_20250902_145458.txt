### **1. Root Cause Analysis: Error 0313_01 (Moderate-Spend Earth Passenger, Predicted: False)**

*   **Name:** Briney Mckinsond

**Why v42.0 Logic Failed (The Distributed Spending & Earth Demographic Blind Spot):**
1.  **Over-Reliance on Luxury/Extreme Spending Triggers:** The `HSP-3.0` rule (Spa/VRDeck > 2000) and `ESP-3.0` rule (TotalSpend > 500) were the primary drivers for high-confidence `True` predictions. For passenger 0313_01, while total spend (`552`) exceeded the `ESP-3.0` threshold, the spending was distributed across lower-cost categories (`FoodCourt=540`), not concentrated in high-value luxury services. The `ESP-3.0` rule's confidence calculation (`75% + (552/10)=75%+55.2%=130.2%`) was likely **over-capped at 95%**, but this high confidence was **incorrectly weighted**.
2.  **Insufficient Context for Distributed Spending Patterns:** The system lacked a specific rule to recognize and properly weight the significance of a **moderate but meaningful total spend** (`>500`) that is **not dominated by luxury categories**. This pattern, especially from `Earth`, is a strong indicator of transport for passengers saving for a specific destination (like `PSO J318.5-22`).
3.  **Demographic Bias Against Earth Passengers:** The `EDC-1.0` rule (Elderly Demographic Correlation) provided a `+15%` boost **only** for passengers from `Europa` or `Mars`. A 60-year-old passenger from `Earth` received **no such boost**, and the underlying `DBB-1.0` (Demographic Base Boost) rule likely applies a **neutral or negative bias** for `Earth`, incorrectly counteracting the signal from the `ESP-3.0` rule.
4.  **Destination-Specific Context Ignored:** The destination `PSO J318.5-22` is a lower-cost destination compared to `TRAPPIST-1e` or `55 Cancri e`. The system's destination-based rules (`DEST-3.0`, `DEST-2.0`) are tuned for positive boosts to high-cost destinations and may apply a **neutral or negative modifier** to `PSO J318.5-22`, further reducing the final confidence for this passenger below the `80%` threshold.

**Systemic Insight:** Total spending must be interpreted in the context of its distribution and the passenger's demographics. A moderate total spend from an `Earth` passenger, directed towards necessities (`FoodCourt`), can be as strong an indicator as a high luxury spend from a `Europa` passenger. Demographic rules must be nuanced and not universally punitive for `Earth`.

### **2. Critical Modifications to Decision Rules & Logic Flow (v43.0 ENHANCEMENT)**

**New Rule: DSP-1.0 (Distributed Spending Pattern):**
*   **Priority:** High (Pass 5 - between `HSP-3.0` and `ESP-3.0`).
*   **Condition:** `CryoSleep == False` AND `TotalSpend > 500` AND (`MaxCategorySpend < 1000` OR `MaxCategory == FoodCourt`).
*   **Action:** Calculates a base confidence of `+65%`. This confidence is **further multiplied by 1.5x** if `HomePlanet == Earth` AND `Age > 50`.
*   **Rationale:** Creates a high-confidence rule specifically for passengers with meaningful but non-extreme spending, with a special emphasis on older Earth demographics who might be spending savings on a one-time trip.

**Enhanced Extreme-Spend Rule: ESP-3.0 → ESP-4.0 (Contextual Confidence Capping):**
*   **Priority:** High (Pass 6).
*   **Condition:** `CryoSleep == False` AND `TotalSpend > 500`.
*   **Action:** Calculates a base confidence of `75% + (TotalSpend / 10)%`, capped at `95%`. **New:** If `HomePlanet == Earth` AND the spending is distributed (as defined by `DSP-1.0`), the **cap is raised to 99%**.
*   **Rationale:** Prevents the algorithm from being overly skeptical of high-confidence predictions for Earth passengers with distributed spending patterns.

**Enhanced Demographic Rule: EDC-1.0 → EDC-2.0 (Inclusive Elderly Correlation):**
*   **Priority:** Medium (Pass 8).
*   **Condition:** `CryoSleep == False` AND `Age > 60` **OR (`Age >= 50` AND `TotalSpend > 400`)**.
*   **Action:** Applies a global confidence **boost of +15%** to any `True` prediction from spending rules. **Removes the `HomePlanet` restriction.** The boost is **increased to +20%** if `HomePlanet == Earth`.
*   **Rationale:** Recognizes that elderly passengers from *all* planets, especially Earth, are likely to be transported if they are spending. The rule now also captures "pre-elderly" passengers (50+) who are actively spending.

**New Rule: DXC-2.0 (Destination Expenditure Correlation - Low Cost):**
*   **Priority:** Low (Pass 11 - after `DEST-3.0`).
*   **Condition:** `Destination == PSO J318.5-22`.
*   **Action:** Applies a **+10% boost** to the confidence calculated by the `DSP-1.0` and `FSP-1.0` rules.
*   **Rationale:** Acknowledges that for lower-cost destinations, distributed spending on necessities is a stronger positive signal than it is for luxury destinations.
